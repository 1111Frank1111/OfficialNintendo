Home Consoles

The Color Tv Series (1977 - 1980)

The line up of consoles that started off the Nintendo’s gaming franchise with a total of 5 consoles. This was the first generation of home consoles. In that note nintendo did not do the second generation. The majority of the consoles are arcade systems remastered from nintendo doing it done before.

Color TV-Game 6 (1977)

It had six variations of Light for the games Tennis or Pong, Hockey and Volleyball. Each one could be played in singles or doubles mode. The consoles had a small run for only a few hundred units.

Color TV-Game 15 (1977)

Nintendo sold over one million Color TV-Game 15 consoles. 15 slightly different versions of Light Tennis also, the paddle controls were now extended apart from the console, and were wired into it. The console came in two colours of orange, the light orange one is much more uncommon.

Color TV-Game Racing 112 (1978)

A bird's-eye-view racing game which has a two player mode as well with controls that could attach on the back of the console and sold 160,000.

Color TV-Game Block Breaker (1979)

A 1-player console runs a ported version of "Block Breaker" , one of Nintendo's arcade games based on Atari's Breakout. The in-game paddle was controlled by a dial attached to the system and there are switches to play variations of the same game, like the Color TV-Game 6.

Computer TV-Game (1980)

Only game in this console is a port of Nintendo's first video arcade game, Computer Othello. The game can be played head-to-head by two people, or against a computer and was quite sophisticated for the time.

NES (1986)

Part of the 3rd generation of consoles. An 8-bit video game console, that was released multiple times underneath the 2 names, the NES, Nintendo Entertainment System, and the famicom, family computer. This console is the best selling console of its time and become the new face of gaming by hitting the market by influence more competitors like Sega, Playstation and Microsoft.
Later, in nintendo’s run in the year 2016 they released the NES Classic Edition which was a remake of the NES. It had 30 games in built to the console and modernised ports.

SNES (1991)

Part of the 4th generation of consoles. A 16-bit game console, that was also released under 2 names, SNES or, Super Nintendo Entertainment System and the Super Famicom or, Super Family Computer. This is one of the first consoles to cheat 3D by have a super FX chip making the visuals appear 3D but on a 2D platformer.
Later this year in september 2017 they did the samething with the NES Classic edition by naming it Super NES Classic Edition and importing famous gaming titles.

N64 (1996)

Part of the 5th generation of consoles. A 64-bit game console, featuring 3D polygon model rendering capabilities and a built-in multiplayer that go up to four players. Introduced the analog stick and the rumble pack to the gaming industry that we know today in every controller. Known for being their major 2d platforming game titles converting over to the 3d gaming world like Super Mario 64, Legend of Zelda and many more.

GCN (2001)

Part of the 6th generation of consoles. Has a similar control to the N64 but c-stick controls are formed into the second Joystick. Is one of the 4 consoles in its race, competing against the PS2, XBOX, and Sega Dreamcast. The second screen controller was innovated by the game boy advanced adapter making it a console compatible with the GBA games. Is the first nintendo console dedicated to having a cd for the optical input for running the games.

Wii (2006)

Introduced motion controls to the gaming world, with Wifi support, SD card slot and Usb slot. Known for its big sales going up to 100 plus million units sold worldwide. The Wii Mini was introduced later in its life span making it designed of it by lacking the gamecube’s backward compatibility which the Wii original did not.

Wii U (2012)

The successor of the wii and the origin of the Nintendo e-shop having it a better compatibility with the internet. It's backwards compatible with the wii games, and accessories with the virtual consoles acting as the compatibilty for the games of Nintendo consoles ever since the NES. Also it's the first console of nintendo to endure the high resolution for 1080p. It also the first console to have a gamepad dedicated to the games that fitted the console, standing at 800p for the resolution type of the gamepad.

Switch (2017)

A hybrid console of the TV Mode and the handheld mode using the tablet as the power for the console. The first nintendo console to use USB type-C as charging port, rather using their own way of charging like the DSI and the 3DS. The switch is the first nintendo console to involve the handheld as a hybrid making the cartridge come back from the N64.
